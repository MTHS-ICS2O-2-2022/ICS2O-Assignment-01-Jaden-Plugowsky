Ada Lovelace


10 December 1815 - 27 November 1852


Contributions


- Ada Lovelace is considered the first computer programmer, this is because she detailed an algorithm specifically created for a machine to perform.

- She wrote about a computer which was never built, the “Analytical Engine”, which she designed with Charles Babbage, an English Polymath. She helped translate a paper written by Luigi Menabrea, an Italian mathematician and politician, on Babbage's Analytical Engine from French to English, and with it, she added many annotations on extra uses for it, along with other notes about it.

- She is credited with being the first person to realise that computers can be used for purposes other than simply to calculate mathematical, such as creating music and art



History


- She was born to Lord and Lady Byron on 10 December, 1815, London, England. She died November 27, 1852, at the very young age of 36 years old, she had died due to Uterine cancer

- She was taught mathematics from a young age, especially after her father left due to an extramarital affair. Her mother wanted her not to follow in her father's footsteps, and so she taught her mathematics to avoid the “romantic ideals” and “moody nature” of her father.

- She met Charles Babbage at the age of 17 through their mutual friend Mary Somerville. He demonstrated to her a portion of his Difference Engine. She became fascinated with the Difference Engine and visited Babbage as much as she could.

- Charles Darwin read her an excerpt from his book “Dombey and Son” while she was at her deathbed. In addition, she was acquainted with Michael Faraday, Sir David Brewster, and Mary Somerville, among others. Before her death, she had requested to be buried next to her father's grave in the Byron family vault, although she had never met her father.
